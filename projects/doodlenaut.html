Doodlenaut

A character customization experience for a game where players shape a character’s personality. We compared two interaction styles—trait selection vs sliders—and measured usability and satisfaction.

UX Research Usability Testing A/B Comparison React

Overview

What it is, who it’s for, and what we were testing.

Product

  • Web-based character customization flow (landing → optional quiz → customization → completion).
  • Designed to be fun + simple, targeted for kids ages 6–12.
  • Built in React with UI styling in CSS.

Question

  • Does a simpler “pick traits” UI feel more usable than a more precise “slider” UI?
  • We compared perceived usability (SUS), ease, satisfaction, and completion time.
  • Mixed-methods: quantitative metrics + qualitative feedback/themes.

Design variations

Two ways to customize personality traits.

Design A — Trait grid

Lower complexity

Users select any 5 traits out of 10 from a grid. Selected traits appear in-order and can be removed.

Selection limit • Add/remove traits • “Done” to finish

Design B — Sliders

Higher precision

Users adjust sliders between opposing traits (e.g., Creative ↔ Analytical) to fine-tune personality.

Granular control • Adjust anytime • “Done” to finish

Results

What we found across usability + effectiveness metrics.

SUS (avg)

Higher is better

Design A: 75
Design B: 82

Design B rated more usable overall

Ease (1–7)

Higher is easier

Design A: 5.4
Design B: 6.3

Sliders felt easier to customize

Completion time

Lower is faster

Design A: 4.2 min
Design B: 5.1 min

Grid was faster; sliders took longer

Satisfaction (1–7)

Higher is better

Design A: 5.6
Design B: 6.1

Design B slightly more satisfying

Qualitative themes

  • Users liked the art style + overall “fun” feel, but wanted clearer instructions.
  • Some features were confusing without guidance (quiz, sliders for some users).
  • Takeaway: speed isn’t everything—users preferred control + clarity.
